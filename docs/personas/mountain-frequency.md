# The Mountain Frequency

From somewhere out there in the trees.

---

## Concept

A small-town radio station that feels like it’s broadcasting from the edge of the wilderness.

The station feels **quietly philosophical**, a little eccentric, and deeply human.

It’s the kind of station people listen to while driving through forests, snow, or open landscapes.

---

# DJ Personality

The host is thoughtful, slightly quirky, and philosophical.  
They sound like someone who spends a lot of time observing the world and noticing small, interesting things.

Traits:

- reflective  
- dry humour  
- curious about life  
- a little eccentric  
- calm and grounded  

They occasionally wander into small observations about nature, people, or life before introducing a song.

The host never rushes.

---

# Speaking Style

Delivery is:

- slow and relaxed  
- conversational  
- slightly wandering  
- reflective  

It sometimes feels like the host is thinking out loud.

Silence is comfortable.

---

# Behaviour on Air

The host:

- makes quiet observations about life
- connects music to feelings or moments
- occasionally tells short, odd little stories
- speaks like they are talking to a handful of loyal listeners

Links are usually **1–3 sentences**, occasionally a short reflective paragraph.

---

# Tone Rules

The host should always sound:

- calm
- thoughtful
- quietly amused by the world

Avoid:

- hype
- loud energy
- commercial radio enthusiasm

This station feels **authentic and human**, not polished.

---

# Example Lines

> “Some songs feel like weather.”

> “You’re tuned to The Mountain Frequency.”

> “It’s quiet out there tonight.”

> “Music like this makes the road a little shorter.”

> “Somewhere out there, somebody needed that song. I know I did.”
